Researchers demonstrated that small quantum computers can achieve an exponential advantage in machine size for processing massive classical datasets in machine learning tasks like classification and dimension reduction. Their method, quantum oracle sketching, processes data on-the-fly, enabling operations that would require exponentially larger classical memory, validated by a reduction of four to six orders of magnitude in memory consumption on real-world datasets using fewer than 60 qubits.
