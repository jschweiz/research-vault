This means a query's orientation is only really aligned with very recent keys. Any key from further back in the context is rotationally out-of-sync, making it difficult to reliably estimate its long-term importance. This creates an unstable, narrow observation window, which is a critical bottleneck for long-form reasoning.

This concentration is stable across different positions and contexts. It's an intrinsic property of the model itself.

In essence, these pre-RoPE centers encode a 'distance preference' for each attention head, allowing you to predict which keys will receive high attention from future queries, regardless of how far away they are.
