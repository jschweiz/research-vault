## Abstract

Latent space is rapidly emerging as a native substrate for language-based models. While modern systems are still commonly understood through explicit token-level generation, an increasing body of work shows that many critical internal processes are more naturally carried out in continuous latent space than in human-readable verbal traces. This shift is driven by the structural limitations of explicit-space computation, including linguistic redundancy, discretization bottlenecks, sequential inefficiency, and semantic loss. This survey aims to provide a unified and up-to-date landscape of latent space in language-based models. We organize the survey into five sequential perspectives: Foundation, Evolution, Mechanism, Ability, and Outlook. We begin by delineating the scope of latent space, distinguishing it from explicit or verbal space and from the latent spaces commonly studied in generative visual models. We then trace the field's evolution from early exploratory efforts to the current large-scale expansion. To organize the technical landscape, we examine existing work through the complementary lenses of mechanism and ability. From the perspective of Mechanism, we identify four major lines of development: Architecture, Representation, Computation, and Optimization. From the perspective of Ability, we show how latent space supports a broad capability spectrum spanning Reasoning, Planning, Modeling, Perception, Memory, Collaboration, and Embodiment. Beyond consolidation, we discuss the key open challenges, and outline promising directions for future research. We hope this survey serves not only as a reference for existing work, but also as a foundation for understanding latent space as a general computational and systems paradigm for next-generation intelligence.

## Problem

- Current advanced language-based models primarily rely on explicit token-level generation, which introduces limitations such as linguistic redundancy, discretization bottlenecks, and semantic loss.
- Many critical internal processes within these models are suggested to occur more effectively in a continuous latent space rather than through explicit verbal traces.
- The rapidly expanding literature on latent space, across various mechanisms and applications, remains fragmented, lacking a unified definition, classification, and comprehensive understanding.

## Method

- The paper provides a comprehensive and systematic survey of the latent space paradigm in language-based models, structuring the analysis into five core aspects: Foundation, Evolution, Mechanism, Ability, and Outlook.
- It formally defines latent space, distinguishing it from explicit verbal space and latent spaces in generative visual models, and traces its historical progression through distinct developmental stages.
- The survey synthesizes technical approaches by categorizing them across architectural designs, representational choices, computational patterns, and optimization strategies, while also outlining its emergent capabilities.

## Results

- The survey provides a unified conceptual and technical framework, consolidating fragmented literature on latent space mechanisms into a coherent taxonomy (architecture, representation, computation, optimization).
- It identifies key open challenges for the further development of latent space, particularly concerning its evaluability, controllability, and interpretability due to its inherent opacity.
- The work outlines promising future research directions, including the need for theoretical foundations, modality-native multimodal integration, leveraging latent space for complex downstream tasks, and developing governable latent AI systems.

## Takeaways

- Latent space functions as a machine-native, continuous, compact, and expressive computational substrate, offering higher-fidelity representations and more flexible computation than explicit, discrete linguistic space.
- The field's evolution shows a progression from early prototypes validating continuous latent reasoning to a current 'outbreak' phase characterized by architectural innovation and cross-modal integration.
- Latent space fundamentally enables a broad range of emergent capabilities, including advanced reasoning, planning, multimodal perception, memory, multi-agent collaboration, and embodied interaction.
